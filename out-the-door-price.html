How AZ Taxes Actually Work

✅ Arizona private-party exemption — a real money-saver When you buy from a private seller within Arizona, you pay zero state sales tax (TPT). This can save $1,400+ on a $25,000 vehicle versus buying the same car at a dealer.
What you still pay on private sales ›

Even with no sales tax, private-party purchases still require:

Vehicle License Tax (VLT) — AZ's registration fee based on your vehicle's original MSRP and age. Paid annually to ADOT/MVD.

Title transfer fee — approximately $4–$10 at the MVD.

Registration base fee — approximately $8–$15 depending on vehicle weight.

Total registration fees are typically $50–$100 for most used vehicles, in addition to VLT.

Can I avoid tax by doing a "private sale" with a dealer? ›

No. The exemption only applies to transactions with non-licensed individuals. If a dealer, wholesaler, or any licensed motor vehicle dealer is involved, the TPT applies regardless of how the paperwork is structured.

⚠️ Dealer sales are subject to TPT — and the rate depends on city Arizona's Transaction Privilege Tax stacks state (5.6%) + county + city rates. Phoenix buyers pay roughly 8.6% total; some municipalities hit 9–11%.

Trade-in tax credit — dealer only ›

At a licensed Arizona dealer, your trade-in value is subtracted from the purchase price before TPT is calculated. Trade a $10k vehicle toward a $28k purchase and you only pay tax on $18k — saving roughly $1,500+ at Phoenix rates.

This credit does not apply if you sell your old vehicle separately (even on the same day). It only applies when the trade-in goes directly to the dealer.

Vehicle License Tax (VLT) — AZ's annual registration fee The VLT is calculated on the original MSRP of the vehicle, depreciated each year. It's your largest annual fee and is often a surprise for buyers used to flat registration fees.

The formula:

Assessed Value = MSRP × 60% × (1 − 0.1625)^(years since new)
VLT = (Assessed Value ÷ 100) × $2.89
Minimum VLT = $10

⚠️ Out-of-state purchase? You pay AZ use tax at registration. Even buying from a private seller in Nevada, California, or another state, you'll owe AZ's 5.6% use tax when you title the vehicle here. The private-party exemption only applies to in-state private sales.
What if I paid sales tax in the other state? ›

Arizona provides a credit for sales tax you paid to another state. You'll owe the difference if AZ's rate (5.6%) is higher than what you paid. If you paid more tax in the other state, you generally get no refund — but you won't be double-taxed beyond 5.6% total.

Oregon, Montana, Alaska, New Hampshire, and Delaware have no state sales tax — so any vehicle purchased there and registered in AZ will owe the full 5.6% AZ use tax.

Montana LLC loophole — don't do it ›

Montana has no sales tax and no registration requirement for vehicles owned by Montana LLCs. Some buyers form Montana LLCs specifically to register high-dollar vehicles there and avoid home-state taxes. This is legal in Montana — but constitutes tax evasion in your home state if you're an AZ resident using the vehicle primarily in AZ. ADOT actively investigates this and penalties include back taxes, interest, and fines.

Use Arizona's official Vehicle Use Tax Calculator for out-of-state purchases — it's the authoritative tool.
