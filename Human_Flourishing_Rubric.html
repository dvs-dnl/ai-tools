About This Rubric — Frameworks & Sources
Aristotle — Eudaimonia (4th c. BCE) The foundational idea that flourishing is not merely feeling good but living and acting in accordance with one's highest capacities — a complete, excellent human life.
Martin Seligman — PERMA Model (2011) Positive psychology's framework: Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment as the five pillars of wellbeing.
Martha Nussbaum — Capabilities Approach (2011) Flourishing requires a threshold of real capabilities — bodily health, emotional life, affiliation, practical reason, play, and control over one's political environment, among others.
Self-Determination Theory — Ryan & Deci (2000) Psychological wellbeing depends on the fulfillment of three basic needs: autonomy (self-authorship), competence (mastery), and relatedness (connection).
Bhutan — Gross National Happiness (1972) A policy framework measuring wellbeing across living standards, health, education, governance, ecological diversity, time use, psychological wellbeing, cultural resilience, and community vitality.
OECD Better Life Index (2011) A comparative framework spanning eleven domains: housing, income, jobs, community, education, environment, civic engagement, health, life satisfaction, safety, and work-life balance.
